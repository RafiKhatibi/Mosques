The History of the 1st Mosque in Islam

Masjid al-Haram is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of unity for Muslims worldwide. It serves as a gathering point during the Hajj pilgrimage, which is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, making it a vital aspect of the Islamic faith Masjid al-Haram, also known as the Sacred Mosque or the Great Mosque of Mecca, is located in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. It is recognized as the holiest site in Islam and is the largest mosque in the world, covering an area of approximately 356,800 square meters (88.2 acres).

To locate Masjid al-Haram on a map, you can follow these simple steps:

Use Google Maps: Open Google Maps. In the search bar, type "Masjid al-Haram" or "Great Mosque of Mecca" and press Enter. This will direct you to the location on the map.
Mobile Apps: If you are using a smartphone, you can use the Google Maps app or any other map application (like Apple Maps). Simply enter "Masjid al-Haram" in the search function of the app.
